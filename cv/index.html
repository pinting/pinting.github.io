I was born in Hungary in 1996. Throughout my elementary years, I have got involved in web development which pushed my life into the direction of becoming a software engineer. In 2014, during my senior year in high school, I was hired to develop an instant messaging system for a startup company. Later on, the company declared bankruptcy, so I had to move on. In 2015, I started studying computer engineering in Budapest. During this period I was focusing on enterprise application development - which became handy later when I had to cover my growing financial needs. However, after exploring the Hungarian contemporary scene of artists, I realized I cannot follow the ideology that was broadcasted by my university any longer. I wanted a different life, I wanted to contribute to an idea, thus I left the campus and became a full-time employee at Accedo. As a result of my stabilized state of being, I started to invest more time into video game development. My first project in this new field was a web-based 2D game engine, called RoboLand. At that point, I decided to leave Europe and take a trip to Yogyakarta, Indonesia to clear my mind. However, I did not want to abandon my job, I had to plan with continuing my work - but I did not want to return to Budapest - so I planned ahead and requested a relocation. This is how I could transfer to Guadalajara, Mexico at the beginning of 2020. While working there during the time of the COVID-19 pandemic, an idea formed in my mind - to abstract interactive scenes using only photographic input. So I created a concept of a game that is now called FreakShow. It tries to pull the player into the emotions I felt as an individual of an Eastern European state. My manifesto is to integrate different fields of art through interactive multimedia projects.
